# Task 5 – Part A: Cybercrime Case Study

## Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common cybercrime in India where scammers trick users into authorizing payments or revealing sensitive details. It usually begins when a victim receives a phone call, SMS, or message posing as a bank official, customer care executive, or even a buyer/seller from platforms like OLX. The fraudster creates urgency — claiming issues like "KYC update required" or "payment pending."

---

## How It Happens – Step by Step

1. The attacker contacts the victim pretending to be trustworthy.
2. They request the victim to install a remote access app (like AnyDesk) or click a malicious link.
3. The victim is asked to "receive money" by approving a UPI request, but this actually **debits** their account.
4. In some cases, OTPs or UPI PINs are extracted under false pretenses.
5. Once access is gained, money is quickly transferred to multiple accounts.

---

## Who Is Targeted?

- Students and young adults unfamiliar with digital payment risks
- Elderly people with limited digital literacy
- Individuals who frequently use online marketplaces (OLX, Facebook Marketplace, etc.)

---

## Consequences

The consequences can be severe, including:

- Immediate financial loss
- Emotional stress and anxiety
- Difficulty recovering funds (recovery depends on how quickly the victim reports to the bank or cybercrime authorities)
- In many cases, victims realize the fraud only **after** the transaction is completed

---

## Key Takeaway

This case highlights the importance of digital awareness. **Never share OTPs or UPI PINs**, and always verify the authenticity of any request before taking action.
